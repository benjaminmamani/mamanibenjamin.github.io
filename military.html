Military dictatorship.

Between 1973 and 1990, Chile lived under a dictatorship known as the Military Regime. The leader of that dictatorship was Augusto Pinochet, Commander-in-Chief of the Chilean Army who carried out a coup to overthrow President Salvador Allende.

Allende had won the 1970 elections with a coalition of left-wing parties that promoted a political and economic system that combined socialist and communist ideas. The new government promoted the nationalization of private companies that managed basic services such as water or electricity, for example.

However, a part of society did not see well the socialist policies of Allende, such as businessmen, the wealthy classes or the military. They considered their communist influence a danger.

At that time, the United States government was competing with the Soviet Union to impose its political system. For this reason, the Central Intelligence Agency (CIA) of the United States financed a coup against Allende.

On September 11, 1973, the military rose up and entered the Palacio de la Moneda, the seat of government. Allende committed suicide in his office when the military entered the palace.
